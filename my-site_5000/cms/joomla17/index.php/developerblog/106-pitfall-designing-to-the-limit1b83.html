Pitfall, designing to the limit

Last Updated on Friday, 27 January 2012 17:30 | Written by Super User | | Hits: 1027

Famed game designer David Crane talks about his design challenges creating Pitfall for the Atari 2600. He practically has put the console to its limit nicely with his brilliant game idea, and this surely had brought one of the best gaming moments to many people, including me.

I think the video is worth watching. The presentation may seem a little bit technical for non programmers. Overall, I think this could be good for anyone, any roles in Game development. 
 
quote from Wiki
...
I sat down with a blank sheet of paper and drew a stick figure in the center. I said, “Okay, I have a little running man and let's put him on a path (two more lines drawn on the paper]. Where is the path? Let's put it in a jungle draw some trees. Why is he running [draw treasures to collect, enemies to avoid, etc.) And Pitfall! was born. This entire process took about ten minutes. About 1,000 hours of programming later, the game was complete.
...
 
The game has been crafted perfectly from his clear knowledge on the target platform, with the solid game idea
 
About 15 minutes in, he says "We couldn't just say 'I want a helicopter', or 'I want the ocean'.  You had to say 'What can the Atari 2600 do, and based on what it can do, what do I want?'".  This is still true now - the hardware has become hugely more powerful, but it still has limits, and a good designer will understand those limits and work with them.
